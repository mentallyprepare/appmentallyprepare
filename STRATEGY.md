# Mentally Prepare — Complete Product Strategy & Build Blueprint

---

## Executive Summary

**Mentally Prepare is an anonymous emotional-preparation and reflection app, not a social network or a therapy platform.** Users enter a quiet space where they prepare emotionally for real situations they are facing — a difficult conversation, an exam, moving to a new city — through guided private writing, and optionally share their reflection with one anonymous peer who is preparing for something similar.

It is the difference between "talk to a stranger for 21 days" and "I have something real happening tomorrow and I need to prepare for how it might feel."

The shift from a 21-day pen-pal commitment to situation-based emotional preparation dramatically increases relevance, lowers the barrier to entry, and gives the product a clear, defensible category: emotional preparation, not anonymous chatting, not therapy, not journaling alone.

## 1. Current Concept Audit

| Assumption | Evidence For | Evidence Against | Risk | Recommendation | Test Required |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 21-day writing commitment is sustainable | Some users completed cycles | Most users won't commit to 21 days with a stranger they haven't met | **High** — cold-start, churn before value | Shorten to 1-7 day situation-based loops | A/B test 3-day vs 7-day vs 21-day commitment |
| Archetypes add value | Users enjoy personality frameworks | Labeled "Protector/Connector/Performer/Disconnector" feels diagnostic, permanent, and clinically loaded | **Medium** — could alienate or mislead | Replace with temporary emotional states ("right now I feel...") not identity labels | User test whether archetypes feel like a cage or a mirror |
| Anonymous matching is safe | Built-in block/report, mutual reveal | 7 Cups proves anonymous peer support attracts bad actors faster than moderation can scale | **High** — existential safety risk | Keep matching 1:1 only, no chat, only written reflection exchange with 24h delay, no real-time | Run safety red-team exercise |
| Silent Room adds value | Users posted emotional lines | No replies means no connection — it's a broadcast, not an interaction | **Medium** — nice-to-have, not core | Keep as secondary feature, not part of onboarding | Test whether users return for Silent Room alone |
| College students want this | 57% of students report loneliness, campus counselling is overstretched | Students may prefer real-world social connection or proven tools | **Low-Medium** — audience exists but intent to use is unproven | Start with 18-25, validate willingness to use before building | Landing-page signup test |
| Product is clearly understood | The website copy is emotionally resonant | The 21-day commitment is the headline but it's a big ask | **Medium** — positioning confusion | Simplify: "Prepare for what's ahead. Write to yourself. Share with someone who gets it." | 5-second test of homepage |

**Direct verdict**: The core emotional insight is strong — people want to be seen without performing. But the 21-day fixed structure, permanent archetype labels, and heavy anonymity create friction, anxiety, and safety surface area without clear corresponding value. The concept needs simplification, not expansion.

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary Beachhead: 18–25 year olds in India (urban, college or early career)

**Why India first:**
- 57% of college students report loneliness (Trellis Strategies 2026, US data; Indian rates likely similar or higher given collectivist culture paradox)
- Smartphone penetration in 18-25 bracket exceeds 85% in urban India
- College counselling is virtually nonexistent at most Indian institutions
- Anonymous apps (Tellonym, NGL, Reddit) already popular in this demographic — the behavior is established
- Lower CAC than US, less competitive landscape
- Founder understands the cultural context

### Ideal User Profile

- **Age**: 19–23
- **Life situation**: College student or first-job professional in a metro or tier-2 city
- **Emotional situation**: Performing fine publicly, feels disconnected privately. Has things they want to say but no one they trust to say them to.
- **Current alternatives**: Overthinking alone, venting on anonymous Instagram confessions, ignoring it
- **Frustrations**: "Everyone expects me to be okay." "My friends will judge me if I admit I'm struggling with something small." "I don't want to worry my parents."
- **Triggering moment**: Failed an exam, had a fight with a parent, feeling behind peers, can't sleep because of something tomorrow
- **What they're afraid of**: Being seen as weak, burdening others, being laughed at
- **What they want but can't say publicly**: "I'm scared I'm not good enough." "I don't know what I'm doing with my life."
- **Why they'd download tonight**: Something happened today. They need to process it. They can't talk to anyone they know.
- **What would make them delete it**: Feeling judged. Being recognized. Getting unwanted messages. Feeling more alone after using it.
- **What would bring them back tomorrow**: Their anonymous peer wrote back. They have a situation coming up they need to prepare for. Their streak of honest writing feels meaningful.

## 4. Jobs to Be Done

### Primary Job: "Help me process what's coming so I can face it with more clarity."

- **Trigger**: Something real is happening tomorrow/this week — an interview, a conversation, a decision
- **Desired progress**: Go from anxious/spinning/numb to clear/grounded/prepared
- **Current alternative**: Overthink alone, vent to a friend who doesn't really get it, avoid it entirely
- **Pain with alternative**: Alone = spiral. Venting = regret or burden. Avoidance = anxiety grows
- **Moment of value**: The first time you write something honest and realize you understand your own feelings better
- **Success condition**: User reports "I felt more ready for that situation because I prepared"

### Secondary Job: "Help me feel less alone without having to perform."

- **Trigger**: Evening loneliness, feeling like no one understands your specific situation
- **Desired progress**: From isolated to "someone else is in something real too"
- **Current alternative**: Scroll Instagram, watch something, open an anonymous confession page
- **Pain**: Social media makes it worse. Confession pages are toxic.
- **Moment of value**: Opening the app and seeing a notification that your peer wrote back
- **Success condition**: User returns after 7 days because of the peer connection

### Tertiary Job: "Help me understand what I'm actually feeling."

- **Trigger**: You're off but can't name it. Something feels heavy.
- **Desired progress**: From vague unease to named emotion
- **Current alternative**: Ignore it, distract, wait for it to pass
- **Pain**: It doesn't pass. Unexamined feelings compound.
- **Moment of value**: Completing a guided check-in and seeing a pattern you hadn't noticed
- **Success condition**: User identifies an emotion they couldn't name before

## 5. Feature Architecture

### P0 — MVP (Build First)

| Feature | User Need | Behaviour | Why Essential | Complexity | Safety Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Guided preparation flow** | "I have something coming and need to process it" | Step-by-step: Name the situation → How do I feel about it? → What am I afraid might happen? → What do I need right now? → Private summary | Core product — this IS the product | Medium | Low — private writing, no sharing |
| **Private journal archive** | "I want to keep what I wrote" | All entries saved chronologically, searchable by situation tag | Retention — users build a library of preparation moments | Low | Low |
| **One-to-one anonymous peer reflection** | "I want someone to witness what I wrote without judging" | User opts to share their preparation note. System matches with one peer in similar situation (not exact, similar). Peer reads your note, writes their own about their own situation. No chat, only exchange of reflections. 1 exchange pair at a time. | Core differentiator — witnessed preparation | High | **High** — requires aggressive safety |
| **Situation tagging** | "I want to prepare for my specific thing" | Tag your entry: conversation, exam, decision, change, something hard | Organizes the archive, powers matching | Low | Low |
| **Anonymous identity** | "I don't want anyone to know who I am" | Auto-generated pseudonym, no real name, no photo, no location beyond region | Trust — without this, no one writes honestly | Medium | Low |
| **Safety system** | "I need to feel safe here" | Report any note, automatic crisis keyword detection, block peer, exit 1:1 without explanation, mandatory safety resources on flagged content | **Existential requirement** | Medium | Low (reduces risk) |

### P1 — After Product-Market Evidence

- **Emotional check-in** (daily mood with "I don't know" option)
- **Unsent letter** (write to someone you can't or shouldn't send it to)
- **Scheduled preparation** (set a reminder before a specific event)
- **Pattern recognition** (your preparation themes over time)
- **Silent Room** (one-line anonymous release with 7-day expiry)
- **Mutual reveal** (opt-in identity sharing after 3+ exchanges, not forced)

### P2 — Experimental

- **Voice journaling** (speak your preparation instead of typing)
- **AI prompt personalization** (suggest prompts based on situation type)
- **Guided breathing before writing** (optional 30-second settle)
- **Post-event reflection** (after the thing happened, how did it go?)

### Do Not Build

- **Real-time chat** — creates harassment surface area, destroys reflection quality
- **Public profiles or feeds** — turns app into social network, kills honesty
- **Archetype labels** — replace with temporary emotional state descriptors
- **Community arenas** — moderation burden > value, creates toxicity vectors
- **AI therapist/companion** — false clinical authority, liability, Wysa/7 Cups failures prove the risk
- **Gamification (streaks, points, leaderboards)** — shame-based retention, antithetical to the product's emotional DNA
- **Photo/video sharing** — privacy risk, moderation nightmare
- **Followers/friends lists** — the entire point is that this ISN'T social media

### Dangerous or High-Risk

- **AI emotional analysis of user writing** — you don't want to be wrong about someone's mental state
- **Automated matching without human oversight** — must have manual review capability
- **Under-18 access without parental system** — legal exposure, safety risk
- **Therapist directories** — 7 Cups lawsuit risk (ghost networks)
- **Any feature that promises or implies clinical benefit**

## 6. Matching Model

### Design Principles

1. **Situation-based, not personality-based**: Match on what someone is preparing for, not who they "are"
2. **Asynchronous, not real-time**: Exchange written reflections with 6-24h delay. No "online now" indicators.
3. **Opt-in, not default**: User must actively choose to share their reflection after writing it privately
4. **One at a time**: Only one active peer connection. Complete or close before matching again.
5. **Exit anytime**: Close the connection without explanation. No pressure to continue.

### Matching Flow

1. User writes a private preparation entry (P0 feature)
2. After writing, optional: "Share this with someone who's preparing for something too?"
3. If yes, entry is queued with its situation tag
4. System finds another queued entry with compatible (not identical) situation
5. Both users receive: "Someone read your note. They wrote back about their own situation."
6. Each sees the other's reflection (no way to identify each other)
7. Repeat: After reading, user can write a new reflection to share or close the exchange
8. Exchange ends naturally after 3-5 rounds, or either user can close it anytime

### Failure States

- **No match available**: "No one is preparing for something similar right now. Your note is saved privately. We'll notify you when a match opens." — This is honest, not manipulative.
- **Match doesn't respond**: After 48h, notify: "Your peer hasn't written back. You can wait longer or close this exchange." Auto-close after 5 days.
- **Someone leaves**: "This exchange has ended. Your private entries are still in your journal." No reason needed, no shame.
- **Dependency risk**: Limit exchange to maximum 5 rounds. No ongoing "pen pal" relationships. The product is about preparation, not relationships.
- **Inappropriate content**: Report button on every shared note. Matched user is immediately blocked, content sent for manual review.
- **Self-harm language**: Auto-detect, immediately block the note from being shared, surface crisis resources, flag for manual review. The peer never sees it.

## 9. Retention Design

### Loops (ethical, not addictive)

| Loop | Frequency | Trigger | Emotional Value | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Preparation loop** | Before any real event | User has something coming up | Clarity, readiness | Low — user-initiated, not pushed |
| **Check-in loop** | Daily (optional) | Notification: "How are you right now?" 1x/day max | Self-awareness | Low — one tap to complete |
| **Exchange loop** | Every 1-3 days | Peer wrote back | Witnessed, less alone | Medium — emotional investment |
| **Archive loop** | Weekly | Notification: "This week you prepared for [situation]. How did it go?" | Closure, pattern recognition | Low |
| **Reactivation loop** | After 7 days inactive | "It's been a while. What's been real for you lately?" | Reconnection | Low — not manipulative |

### Notification Strategy

| Notification | Value | Trigger | Max Freq | Opt-out | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Your peer wrote back" | Anticipation, connection | Peer submits note | Per exchange | Yes | Create anxiety if overused |
| "How are you right now?" | Check-in habit | Daily at user-chosen time | 1x/day | Yes | Annoying if user doesn't want it |
| "You prepared for [X] — want to reflect on how it went?" | Closure | 3 days after event date | Per entry | Yes | Could feel like homework |
| "It's been quiet. What's real for you?" | Reactivation | 7 days inactive | 1x/week | Yes | Could feel guilt-inducing |
| "Someone is waiting for your response" | Respect for peer | 24h since they wrote | Per exchange | Yes | Could create pressure |

## 10. Safety Framework

### Age Policy

- **MVP: 18+ only**. No under-18 access. This simplifies safety, legal, and moderation requirements by an order of magnitude.
- Future: If expanding to 13-17, require institutional partnership (school/university) with parental consent, not direct-to-consumer.

### Community Guidelines (Concise, Human)

1. **Write honestly. Don't perform.**
2. **You are not here to fix anyone.**
3. **Do not ask for or share personal information.**
4. **Do not request offline meetings.**
5. **Sexual content, harassment, and hate speech are not permitted.**
6. **If someone says they're in crisis, take it seriously — report it.**
7. **One person at a time. No multiple active exchanges.**

### Moderation System

| Layer | Method | Response Time | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Automated keyword detection** | Regex + basic NLP on shared notes | Real-time | Self-harm, crisis, sexual content, contact info |
| **User reporting** | In-app report on every shared note | < 1 hour for urgent flags | All inappropriate content |
| **Manual review** | Human moderator reviews flagged content | < 24 hours | Confirmed violations |
| **Appeals** | Email-based appeal | < 72 hours | Suspected false flags |

### Emergency Protocol

1. App detects or user reports self-harm/crisis language
2. Note is immediately blocked from sharing (peer never sees it)
3. User sees crisis resources screen (cannot be dismissed without explicit action)
4. Moderator is notified immediately
5. User's ability to start new exchanges is paused for 24 hours
6. Follow-up check-in after 24 hours if user returns

**What we promise**: We will show you resources. We will not contact emergency services without your consent unless legally required. We are not a crisis line. If you are in immediate danger, call emergency services.

### Moderation Service Targets

| Incident Type | Detection | Initial Response | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crisis/self-harm | Real-time (automated) | < 1 min (auto block + resources) | < 1 hour (human review) |
| Harassment | < 1 hour (report) | < 1 hour | < 24 hours |
| Sexual content | < 1 hour (report + auto) | < 1 hour | < 24 hours |
| Contact info sharing | Real-time (auto) | Real-time (auto block) | < 24 hours |
| False report | < 24 hours (review) | < 24 hours | < 72 hours |

## 11. Privacy Framework

| Data Point | Collected? | Retention | Purpose | Deletion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Anonymous pseudonym | Yes | Account lifetime | Identity within app | Full delete |
| Email address | Yes | Account lifetime | Account recovery, subscription | Full delete |
| Age (18+ verification) | Yes (self-declared) | Account lifetime | Age gating | Full delete |
| Journal entries | Yes | Until deleted | Core product | User can delete individually |
| Shared reflections | Yes | Until exchange ends or deleted | Core product | Deleted when exchange ends or user deletes |
| Crisis flags | Yes | 1 year | Safety audit | Anonymized after 1 year |
| IP address | Temporarily | 30 days in logs | Ban evasion prevention | Deleted after 30 days |
| Device token | Yes | Until uninstall | Push notifications | On uninstall |
| Payment data | No (handled by Stripe/App Store) | N/A | Processor handles | N/A |

- **No personal data is used for AI training.** Journal entries are never sent to third-party AI APIs.
- **No advertising.** No ad tracking, no pixels, no data sharing with advertisers.
- **End-to-end encryption is not appropriate** — moderated content requires ability to review flagged items. Encrypt at rest with access controls instead.
- **Data export**: JSON export of all entries available on request.
- **Account deletion**: Immediate, irreversible, all data purged within 30 days.

## 12. Brand & Visual Direction

### Visual Principles

1. **Private first** — The app should feel like a closed notebook, not a stage
2. **Warm, not clinical** — No medical greens or sterile whites. Think reading light at 2am.
3. **Quiet, not loud** — No bouncing animations, no celebratory confetti for writing an entry
4. **Human, not corporate** — Imperfect textures, real warmth, no polished stock imagery
5. **Subtle, not minimal** — There's a difference between "stripped down" and "unfinished"

### Color System

- **Background**: Deep warm charcoal (`#1A1714`), not pure black
- **Surface**: Warm dark (`#252220`), not card-white
- **Primary text**: Warm cream (`#F0EAE0`), not pure white
- **Secondary text**: Muted warm (`#9C948A`)
- **Accent**: Dusty amber (`#C4945E`) — used sparingly for CTAs, never for decoration
- **Warning**: Soft rust (`#B86C5A`) — for safety/crisis contexts
- **Success**: Muted moss (`#7A8F6B`) — for completion moments only
- **Line/border**: Subtle warm stroke (`rgba(240,234,224,0.08)`)

### Typography

- **Primary**: Inter (sans-serif, open-source, excellent readability on mobile)
- **Writing/reflection**: EB Garamond (open-source serif, feels personal, human, written)
- **Monospace (code/technical)**: JetBrains Mono
- **Avoid**: Poppins, Montserrat, SF Pro, Roboto — overused in wellness apps

### Icon & Illustration Direction

- **No human illustrations** — no faces, no bodies, no gestures
- **No animal mascots** — Finch owns this territory
- **Abstract, organic shapes** — ink blots, paper folds, light through fabric
- **Line icons** — thin, consistent stroke, never filled
- **Writing-focused metaphors** — envelopes, paper, ink, candles, windows

### Motion Principles

- **Slow in, slow out** — no snappy spring animations
- **Fade + gentle slide** — never pop or bounce
- **No parallax, no 3D, no glassmorphism**
- **Haptics**: Light tap feedback on submit, soft confirmation on save, no celebration haptics

### What It Should NOT Look Like

- ✗ Purple/blue gradients (generic wellness)
- ✗ Glassmorphism (trendy 2023, already dated)
- ✗ Neon glows (gaming/fintech)
- ✗ Lotus/flower/brain symbols (therapy cliché)
- ✗ Smiling stock photos (fake authenticity)
- ✗ Bubbly/shiny buttons (children's app)
- ✗ Dark mode with pure black backgrounds (harsh, not warm)

## 14. Technical Architecture

### Stack Decision: React Native (Expo) + Node.js + PostgreSQL

Why:
- **React Native (Expo)**: Cross-platform from a single codebase. You already have an Expo project. Maintaining iOS + Android separately is not viable for a solo founder.
- **Node.js/Express**: You already have this. It works. No reason to switch.
- **PostgreSQL** (instead of SQLite): Required for production — concurrent writes, proper migrations, JSON fields for flexible data, better tooling for moderation/admin. SQLite is fine for prototyping but will fail under load with multiple writes.
- **Redis**: For rate limiting, session cache, match queue. Lightweight, cheap.
- **Bull/Redis**: For delayed message delivery, scheduled notifications.

### System Architecture (Text)

```
Mobile App (Expo RN)
    ↓ HTTPS + WebSockets
API Gateway (Express + Helmet + Rate Limit)
    ↓
Services Layer
├── Auth Service (JWT, anonymous sessions)
├── Journal Service (CRUD entries, tags)
├── Matching Service (situation queue, compatibility)
├── Exchange Service (delayed delivery, limits)
├── Safety Service (keyword detection, crisis protocol)
├── Notification Service (push, email)
└── Admin Service (moderation, user management)
    ↓
PostgreSQL (primary) + Redis (cache/queue)
```

### Key Technical Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Auth | JWT + anonymous user IDs | No session storage needed, supports anonymous-first model |
| Real-time | No real-time needed | Delayed delivery by design. WebSockets only for notification updates |
| File storage | None (text-only MVP) | No images, no uploads reduces moderation burden 10x |
| AI | None in MVP | Too risky for direct-to-consumer mental health. Revisit only after safety infrastructure is proven |
| Moderation | Automated keyword detection + human review queue | Must exist on day one |
| Encryption at rest | AES-256 (RDS/Cloud SQL default) | Standard |
| Push notifications | Expo Push Notifications + Firebase (Android APNs bridge) | Already set up |
| Payments | RevenueCat (subscription management) + Stripe | Handles cross-platform billing complexity |
| CI/CD | GitHub Actions (already set up) | Works |

## 15. Data Model (Core Entities)

### User
- `id`: UUID
- `pseudonym`: string (auto-generated)
- `email_hash`: string (for recovery, not stored in plain text)
- `password_hash`: string
- `age_range`: string ("18-24", "25-34", etc.) — self-declared, no DOB
- `region`: string (state/country, for matching proximity)
- `created_at`: timestamp
- `deleted_at`: timestamp (soft delete)
- **Sensitive**: password_hash, email_hash. Encrypted at rest. Not accessible via admin UI by default.

### Journal Entry
- `id`: UUID
- `user_id`: UUID → User
- `entry_type`: enum ("preparation", "check_in", "unsent_letter")
- `situation_tag`: string (nullable)
- `content`: encrypted text
- `emotion_tags`: JSON array of strings
- `is_shared`: boolean
- `match_id`: UUID → Match (nullable)
- `created_at`: timestamp
- `updated_at`: timestamp
- **Retention**: Until user deletes. Anonymized for analytics after 90 days if user inactive.
- **Deletion**: Cascade on user delete. Individual delete allowed.

### Match
- `id`: UUID
- `user1_id`: UUID → User
- `user2_id`: UUID → User
- `status`: enum ("pending", "active", "completed", "closed", "flagged")
- `round_count`: integer (max 5)
- `created_at`: timestamp
- `completed_at`: timestamp (nullable)
- **Retention**: 90 days after completion. Anonymized for safety audit after that.

### Exchange Round
- `id`: UUID
- `match_id`: UUID → Match
- `sender_id`: UUID → User
- `content`: encrypted text
- `is_safety_flagged`: boolean (default false)
- `delivered_at`: timestamp (not created_at — delayed delivery)
- **Retention**: Same as match. Deleted when match is deleted.

### Safety Event
- `id`: UUID
- `user_id`: UUID → User
- `event_type`: enum ("crisis_keyword", "report_filed", "auto_block", "manual_ban")
- `content_snippet`: string (truncated, only for review)
- `action_taken`: string
- `resolved_at`: timestamp (nullable)
- **Retention**: 1 year. Legal hold if required.
- **Access control**: Admin only, logged.

## 20. Growth & Distribution

### Pre-Launch (Month 1-2)

- **Founder content**: Write honestly about building the product on Twitter/X and LinkedIn. The product's emotional honesty should extend to how it's built.
- **Instagram**: Short emotional cards (one-line anonymous quotes with the app's visual identity). No stock photos.
- **Reddit**: r/lonely, r/selfimprovement, r/india — share the problem, not the solution. Build trust before promoting.
- **Waitlist**: Simple landing page with "I have something real coming up" CTA. Count waitlist signups.

### First 100 Users (Month 2-3)

- **Personal outreach**: DM 50 people from target demographic on Reddit/Twitter who have posted about loneliness or emotional struggles. Offer early access personally.
- **Campus seeding**: 5 ambassadors at 5 Indian colleges. Give them a direct link to share with 20 friends each.
- **Referral mechanism**: "Invite a friend who's preparing for something real. You both get 1 extra shared preparation."

### First 1,000 Users (Month 3-4)

- **Emotional quiz share cards**: After completing a preparation, option to share an anonymous card: "I'm preparing for [situation]. 1 in 3 people our age feel this way." — this is not bragging, it's normalizing.
- **Instagram content pivots**: Share user stories (anonymized, with permission) about how preparation helped
- **Campus partnerships**: Work with 1-2 college counselling centers to distribute as a "preparation tool, not therapy"

### Cold-Start Matching Strategy

When there are <100 users, matching cannot work reliably. Solutions:

1. **Manual matching by founder**: For the first 50 users, personally read their shared entry and decide if another user is a good match. This is not scalable but ensures quality and safety during the most vulnerable phase.
2. **"Write to yourself" fallback**: If no match is available, the share button becomes "Write an unsent letter" — still a valuable private activity.
3. **Delayed notification**: "We'll notify you when someone who's preparing for something similar joins." This is honest and builds anticipation.
4. **No empty promise**: Never say "thousands of people are waiting to connect with you." This is how 7 Cups fails.

## 24. Final Recommendation

### Recommended Product

**Mentally Prepare should become an emotional preparation app — not a journaling app, not a social network, not a therapy platform, not a pen-pal service.**

Users come because they have something real happening — a conversation, an exam, a change, something hard. They complete a 4-step guided reflection to understand how they feel about it, what they're afraid of, and what they need. This private writing is the core product.

Optionally — and the option is important — they can share their reflection with one anonymous peer who is also preparing for something. Not to chat. Not to become friends. Just to know that someone else read what they wrote and is sitting with their own real thing.

The 21-day commitment becomes a single-session preparation. The permanent archetypes become temporary emotional states. The anonymous matching becomes situation-based, not personality-based. The public Silent Room becomes a private reflection archive.

### Primary Audience

Urban Indian 18-25 year olds — college students and early-career professionals — who have things happening in their lives they need to process but no one to process them with honestly.

### Core Promise

"Prepare for what's real. You don't have to do it alone — but you can if you want to."

### Core Experience (User Loop)

1. Something real is happening → Open Mentally Prepare
2. Complete a 4-step guided preparation (3 minutes)
3. Read your own summary → feel clearer
4. Optionally share with one anonymous peer
5. Return when the next real thing happens

### MVP Features (Maximum 5)

1. **Guided preparation flow** (4-step, with emotion picker and "I don't know" option)
2. **Private journal archive** (chronological, tagged by situation)
3. **Anonymous peer reflection exchange** (situation-based, 1:1, delayed, max 5 rounds)
4. **Safety system** (automated keyword detection, reporting, crisis resources, block)
5. **Anonymous identity** (auto-generated pseudonym, no real name, no photo)

### Features to Remove (from current concept)

- ✗ 21-day fixed commitment (replace with single-session preparation)
- ✗ Archetypes (Protector/Connector/Performer/Disconnector — replace with temporary emotional states)
- ✗ Silent Room as a primary feature (keep as secondary, add after launch)
- ✗ College/gender/year matching fields (irrelevant for situation-based matching)
- ✗ Quiz/scan as onboarding (replace with first preparation flow)
- ✗ "Reveal" mechanic (too much pressure, replace with simple opt-in identity sharing after multiple exchanges)

### Main Differentiator

**No other app helps you prepare emotionally for specific situations and optionally witness someone else's preparation without turning it into social media.** Journaling apps are solo. Anonymous chat apps are real-time and unsafe. Therapy apps are clinical. Finch is gamified. Stoic is philosophical. Mentally Prepare is the only app that asks "What's real for you right now?" and creates a quiet space to face it.

### Biggest Risk

**Users may not have enough "real situations" to return for.** If preparation is event-driven and events are infrequent, retention will be low. Mitigation: The app must surface smaller, everyday situations (a conversation you're avoiding, a decision you're putting off) and provide a daily check-in option for between events.

### First Validation Test

**Landing page A/B test**: "Emotional preparation" headline vs "Anonymous journaling" headline. Measure email signup rate. If preparation angle wins by 20%+, proceed. If not, the concept needs fundamental rethinking.

### 12-Week Goal

A working iOS/Android app that allows 100-300 users to complete the preparation flow, optionally share with a manually-matched peer, and return for a second preparation within 7 days. All safety systems operational. Zero unaddressed safety incidents.

### Go, Pivot, or Stop

**Go — with conditions.**

The emotional preparation angle is differentiated, defensible, and addresses a real need (57% of students report loneliness; life continuously serves new situations to prepare for). The key unknowns are (a) whether preparation is frequent enough to drive retention and (b) whether anonymous peer exchange can remain safe at scale.

Build the solo experience first (weeks 1-6), validate that users complete the preparation flow and return. Add matching only after solo retention is proven. Do not build matching until the safety system is complete and tested.

If solo retention fails (Day 7 < 20%), the concept needs a pivot — possibly toward a daily check-in + pattern recognition model (like How We Feel) or a guided ritual model (like Stoic).
